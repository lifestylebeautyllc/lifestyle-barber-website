LifeStyle Barber: Website Game Plan & Architecture (Part 2)
1. Color Palette: "Modern PNW Vintage"
The Blend: We are fusing rugged Pacific Northwest elements (deep forest greens, rich leather browns, and charcoal greys) with the modern LifeStyle brand colors (grey violet and dark blue).

The Execution: Charcoal grey and forest green will serve as our foundational background and text colors to establish that masculine, grounded lounge vibe. The leather browns will act as warm accent tones (like borders or dividers), while the grey violet and dark blue will be used strategically for high-impact interactive elements, like the "Book Now" buttons and hover states.

2. Site Structure: "Accessible Minimalism"
The Strategy: A continuous-scroll single-page architecture featuring a "sticky" navigation bar that stays at the top of the screen as the user scrolls.

The "Why": This is the exact sweet spot for your target demographics.

For the tech-savvy locals: A smooth, single-page site feels modern, dynamic, and lightning-fast. It proves you are the forward-thinking barber they know you to be.

For the older clientele: It completely removes friction. They don't have to hunt through complex drop-down menus or get lost on separate pages. They can simply click "Services" or "Book Matt" on the sticky header, and the screen will seamlessly glide down to exactly what they need. It is highly advanced under the hood, but beautifully simple on the surface.

3. Service Hierarchy: "Precision & Tradition"
Tier 1: Technical Mastery (The Hook). We lead with classic scissor cuts and tapered finishes. The copy here will lean into your academic tone, explicitly mentioning your cosmetology background, advanced shear work, texturizing, and weight-removal techniques for complex hair types. We establish total trust and competence immediately.

Tier 2: The Barber Lounge Experience (The Tradition). Right below the cuts, we introduce the traditional staples: the hot towel, straight-razor shaves, and steaming men's facials. This reinforces the classic, relaxing atmosphere of the shop.

Tier 3: The Interpersonal Element. A brief section detailing the vibe of the chair—great conversation, humble confidence, and a space to just breathe.
